Krishna Kaul, popularly known as KR$NA, previously as Krsna and YoungProzpekt, is a prominent Indian rapper celebrated for his exceptional lyrical and technical prowess in the Desi Hip-Hop (DHH) scene. Born on October 4, 1987, in Delhi to a Kashmiri Pandit family, Kaul spent part of his childhood in South London, where he began his journey into rap at the age of fourteen, aiming to fit in with his peers. Kaul's breakthrough came with the release of "Kaisa Mera Desh" in 2010, marking the first Indian hip hop track on YouTube and quickly gaining popularity as one of India's most-watched music videos. Over the years, he continued to make waves with sporadic underground releases until signing with Universal Music in 2013, rebranding himself as KR$NA. His debut album "Sellout" in 2014 garnered attention, especially with the hit single "Last Night," reaching #5 on the Vh1 Music Charts in India. After a hiatus, Kaul returned in 2016 with the Hindi track "Vyanjan," followed by collaborations and signings with DNH Artists (now Kalamkaar) in subsequent years. Notably, he accused IPL and Disney+ Hotstar of plagiarizing his song "Dekh Kaun Aaya Wapas" for the 2020 IPL anthem, sparking controversy and legal action threats. In 2021, Kaul featured on American rapper Hi-Rez's tracks "Crossroads" and "Playground," showcasing his international collaborations. His participation in various projects continued in 2022, including a feature on Karan Aujla's song "Ykwim" and winning the Radio City Freedom Award for Best Indie Collaboration with Brodha-V for their track "Forever." Throughout his career, Kaul has been recognized for his contributions to the DHH genre, earning a reputation as one of its greatest artists. With his distinctive style and impactful presence, KR$NA remains a significant figure in the Indian hip-hop landscape, continually pushing boundaries and leaving a lasting impression on the industry.
